Step 1: Enable Linux (Beta) on ChromeOS

First, we need to enable the Linux development environment on your Chromebook. This gives us access to a full Linux terminal where we can run the installation commands.

Go to your ChromeOS Settings by clicking the clock in the bottom-right corner, then the gear icon. Navigate to "Advanced" → "Developers" → "Linux development environment". Click "Turn On" and follow the setup prompts. This process will download about 1GB of data and take 5-10 minutes to complete.

Note: Make sure you have at least 8GB of free storage before starting this step.
